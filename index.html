Bio

I am a Ph.D. student in the School of Information at the University of Michigan, where I study the design of community-centered Computing Education technologies through the lens of Human-Computer Interaction and Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence. My research focuses on how knowledge is produced, circulated, and legitimized across online discussions and peer networks—particularly in learning communities and sociotechnical systems.

I earned my B.S. in Computer Science and Sociology from Georgia State University, where I was mentored by Anu Bourgeois and Mario Kubek. My undergraduate research centered on near-peer mentorship models and educational applications of natural language processing and machine learning. I also completed internships across industry and academia, including Microsoft, RTX, Clemson University, and various non-profit organizations.

At Michigan, I am mentored by Barbara Ericson and Mark Guzdial as I expand on this interdisciplinary work, with the goal of supporting, discussion-driven learning ecosystems in computing education.
